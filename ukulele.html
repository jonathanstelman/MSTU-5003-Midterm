The Ukulele

History of the Ukulele

The ukulele, or uke for short, is a plucked string instrument in the lute family. In the early 1800s, Portuguese settlers brought small guitar-like instruments, known as the Portuguese machete, to the Hawaiian islands. Over the course of the next century, the instrument evolved into its current form, known for its four lightweight nylon or gut strings. The ukulele spread across the world in the 19th century, and while it is best known as the quintessential instrument of Hawaiian music, the ukulele can be found in a range of musical genres, including rock, classical and jazz.

Parts of the Ukulele

Ukueles come in a range of sizes, from the soprano to the bass, but nearly all ukuleles share these same basic parts.

  • Body
  • Neck
  • Headstock
  • Fretboard
  • Bridge
  • Saddle
  • Nut
  • Strings
  • Soundhole
  • Tuning pegs / tuners
