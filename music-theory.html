Music Theory

Notes and Scales

We perceive sound as a spectrum from low to high. Even though this spectrum is continuous, musical traditions from around the world have long separated continuous frequencies into distinct pitches, or notes. Today, most Western music evenly divides octaves into 12 equal steps, and algorithmically selects 7 of these for form the diatonic scale.

While the diatonic scale is the most widely used scale around the world, it is far from being the only musical scale. Serious Ukulele will teach you all about Western tunings and scales, but we will also teach you about the scales that make up the musical traditions of people from all over the world, from the open scales of Pacific Northwest Indian tribes, to the microtonal sounds of the Kurdish Middle East, to the pentatonic scales of Gagaku Japanese court music.
